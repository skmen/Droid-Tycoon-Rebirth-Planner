Droid Tycoon RB Planner

How to Use

A complete walkthrough of every feature in the planner.

1. Set your rebirth level and cycle

At the top of the planner, pick which cycle you're on (Cycle 1–4 — the game repeats a new droid rotation each cycle) and type your current rebirth level into the RB input. The tool immediately shows what your next rebirth requires: the credit cost, any slot unlock, and the droids you need.

Your rebirth level, cycle, and theme choice are remembered automatically (saved in your browser) — no need to re-enter them next time you visit.

2. Reading the droid requirements

Each required droid shows as a colored pill with its tier and name, for example GOLD BU-4D. The pill's color always matches the droid's tier:

  • Default — plain/white
  • Gold — yellow
  • Diamond — blue
  • Rainbow — gradient
  • Beskar — metallic grey

3. Understanding the keep/sell verdicts

Next to each required droid, a badge tells you what to do with it after completing that rebirth:

✓ SELL — this droid at this tier is never required again this cycle. Safe to sell.

↑ SELL, OR UPGRADE — you can sell it now, but you'll need a higher tier of the same droid at a later rebirth. The note underneath tells you exactly which tier and when (e.g. "you'll need a DIAMOND version by RB 18").

⚠ KEEP — this exact tier is needed again later this cycle. Don't sell it.

4. Checking a specific droid ("Keep or sell?")

Type any droid's name into the search box to get an instant verdict for your current rebirth level and cycle — useful when you're staring at your base deciding whether to sell something that isn't in the immediate next-rebirth list. It checks every remaining rebirth in the cycle, not just the next one.

5. The upcoming requirements table

Below the search box is a full table of every droid your current cycle will require from here on out. You can:

  • Filter by type, rarity, or tier using the dropdowns.
  • Sort by clicking any column header (click again to reverse the order).
  • Expand a row by clicking it to see every rebirth level that droid is needed at.

6. Settings menu

The ☰ button in the top-right opens a small menu with two toggles: dark/light theme, and show/hide ads. Both are remembered for next time.

7. Data accuracy

Rebirth requirements are community-sourced and may shift as the game updates. The full data set is published on the RB Requirements page so anyone can cross-check it — if something looks wrong, please let us know. Always verify at the in-game Rebirth Station before selling.
